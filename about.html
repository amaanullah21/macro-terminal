System Methodology

The Macro Terminal is a proprietary cross-asset factor model. It transforms raw economic indicators into standardized Z-scores to project asset class expectancy and optimize portfolio weights via a non-linear softmax engine.

Macro Glossary & Definitions

01. Input Variables

Fed Funds Rate (r) The target interest rate set by the central bank. It represents the nominal cost of liquidity in the financial system.
Neutral r* The "equilibrium" interest rate where the economy is at full employment with stable inflation. It is the pivot point for policy stance.
Inflation (CPI) The rate of increase in prices. High inflation erodes bond returns but can act as a tailwind for commodities.

02. Quantitative Engine

Z-Score Normalization A statistical measure that describes a value's relationship to the mean of a group of values, measured in terms of standard deviations.
Monetary Stance / Gap The difference between the actual rate (r) and the neutral rate (r*). If r > r*, policy is "restrictive."
Interaction Term A variable that captures the compound effect of two factors (e.g., Growth × Inflation) to model non-linear market behaviors.

03. Market Regimes

Expansion (Goldilocks) Positive growth with manageable inflation. Typically the optimal environment for Equities.
Stagflation The combination of stagnant economic growth and high inflation. Highly punitive for traditional 60/40 portfolios.
Overheating Growth is significantly above trend, leading to demand-pull inflation and imminent central bank tightening.

04. Portfolio Math

Softmax Optimization A mathematical function that turns a vector of "expectancy scores" into a probability distribution (portfolio weights) that sums to 100%.
Asset Expectancy The modelled "attractiveness" of an asset based on its historical beta to the current Z-score factor loadings.
Convexity (Fixed Income) The measure of the curvature in the relationship between bond prices and bond yields. It indicates how bond price sensitivity changes as yields move.

Proprietary Allocation Logic

The final weight $w$ for any asset $i$ is determined by the Softmax of the Factor Matrix $R$:

R_i = \beta_{g}Z_{g} + \beta_{\pi}Z_{\pi} + \beta_{L}Z_{L} + \text{Interaction Terms}

Where $Z$ represents the Z-scored macro factor and $\beta$ represents the sensitivity coefficient calibrated to institutional benchmarks.
